History

Franchise History
Sunrisers Hyderabad replaced the Deccan Chargers in 2012 and debut in 2013. The team was announced in Chennai on 18 December 2012. The team is owned by Sun TV Network who won the bid of ₹ 850.5 million per year (approximately $15.9 million) for a five-year deal, a week after Deccan Chargers was terminated due to prolonged financial issues.
Initial Years
Sunrisers Hyderabad made their IPL debut and replaced Deccan Chargers. Kumar Sangakkara has captained the Sunrisers Hyderabad for 9 Matches and Cameron White has captained the team for the remaining 7 matches and the Eliminator match in the Playoffs. In their inaugural season, the team reached the playoffs but were eliminated after losing the eliminator match against Rajasthan Royals by 4 wickets
Maiden title and Success
For the 2015 season, SRH retained 13 players and released 11 players. David Warner was appointed as the SRH captain for this season and led the team in all the matches For the 2016 season, SRH retained 15 players and released 9 players. After auction SRH Traded two players.[23] Sunrisers Hyderabad were crowned champions defeating Royal Challengers Bangalore in the finals and ended the season with 11 wins and 6 losses. This was their maiden title. Bhuvneshwar Kumar became the first Sunrisers Hyderabad player to win Purple Cap.

Meet the Players

David Warner

Batsman

One of the most destructive hitters in world cricket, Warner opens for Australia in all three formats and in 2015 his seniority was recognised when he was named vice-captain to Steven Smith in the Test and ODI sides.

Kane Williamson

Batsman

Kane Williamson is a vital cog in the New Zealand line-up across formats. He has received high praise from none other than the legendary former NZ captain, the late Martin Crowe for his batting abilities.

Bhuvneshwar Kumar

Bowler

It has been a rapid rise to the top for Bhuvneshwar Kumar. He has been the go-to man for Sunrisers bowling at the start of the innings and has been impressive at death as well.

Manish Pandey

Batsman

It took one spectacular innings to thrust Manish Pandey into the limelight. Pandey scored an unbeaten 114 for Bangalore against the Deccan Chargers in IPL 2009. He became the first Indian to score a ton in the IPL.

Shikhar Dhawan

Batsman

Shikhar Dhawan’s powerful batting and great fielding skills make him a perfect fit for the Twenty20 format. The left-hand batsman played for the Delhi in IPL 2008 and was their third-highest run-getter with 340 runs in 14 matches

Rashid Khan

Bowler

From playing tennis ball cricket in the streets of Nangrahar province in war-torn Afghanistan to bagging a lucrative Indian Premier League deal, the journey has not been short of a fairytale for Rashid Khan.

Yusuf Pathan

All-rounder

Yusuf Pathan first made his mark as a hard-hitting batsman and off-spinner for the Baroda U-16 team. His ability to score runs quickly earned him a spot in India's squad for the inaugural World T20 in 2007.

Deepak Hooda

Batsman

A key performer for the India Under-19 side over the last couple of years, Deepak Hooda made his IPL debut with the RR. The off-spinner and batsman was born in Haryana, but plays his domestic cricket for Baroda.

Shakib Al Hasan

All-rounder

A talented left-hand batsman and left-arm spinner, Shakib Al Hasan is one of the best cricketers that Bangladesh has produced. As a bowler, he is accurate, consistent, and canny

Sandeep Sharma

Bowler

The right-arm medium pacer from Punjab, Sandeep Sharma rose to prominence during the 2012 Under-19 ICC World Cup. His four wickets in the final propelled India to the title.

Mohammad Nabi

All-rounder

A sturdy batsman and an off-spinner, Mohammad Nabi can easily be called the pillar of Afghanistan cricket.He played a phenomenal role in the nation's rise in cricket from the 5th Division League to qualifying for the World Cup

Alex Hales

Batsman

Alex Hales is an English cricketer. He is a right-handed batsman and a right-arm medium-pace bowler who currently plays for Nottinghamshire, while also a regular in the England T20 International side.

Wriddhiman Saha

wicket-keeper

This wicket-keeper batsman from Bengal has plied his skills for KKR and CSK before being bought by KXIP in the IPL Player Auction. At times, he was inducted into the national side despite MS Dhoni keeping wickets.

Billy Stanlake

Bowler

The 22-year-old fast bowler from Australia, Billy Stanlake has recently made his debut in limited overs cricket. The extremely tall fast bowler was Australia U19s most economical bowler in the 2014 U19 World Cup.

Siddarth Kaul

Bowler

A right-arm pacer, Siddarth Kaul, was the second highest wicket-taker in the Ranji Trophy 2012-13 season. The youngster was part of the Indian team that won the 2008 Under-19 World Cup in Malaysia.

Carlos Brathwaite

All-rounder

One of the biggest buys for DD ahead of the 2016 edition of the IPL, Carlos Brathwaite made headlines for the first time when he smashed sixes off four consecutive deliveries to help West Indies clinch the World T20 2016 title.
